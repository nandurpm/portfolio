What CNC Operations Taught Me About Precision

2026-05-12 5 min read

Working on CNC machines taught me that precision isn't just about numbers—it's about process discipline, verification, and humble attention to detail.

Tolerance Stack-Up

Individual part tolerances compound across assemblies. You learn quickly that tight tolerances on the wrong dimension create impossible assembly conditions.

Tool Wear and Compensation

Tools change the part dimensions as they wear. Tracking tool life, predicting wear rates, and compensating before failure becomes a practiced skill.

First-Piece Verification

Before running a production batch, you measure every critical dimension on the first completed part. This discipline catches setup errors before they cost money.

Documentation as Insurance

Detailed setup sheets and inspection records protect both quality and your credibility. When problems arise, documentation shows you followed procedure.

The Engineering Connection

CNC experience directly influences how I draw parts and specify tolerances. Understanding what manufacturing can and cannot reliably achieve makes me a better designer.
